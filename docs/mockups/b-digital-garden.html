I looked at 12 months of Shopify data, audited every product page, and compared Mad Hudson against 11 other watch brands. Strong product, strong brand, genuine audience. The gap is in how the site talks to search engines — and most fixes are invisible to customers.

The biggest levers are product content expansion (product pages currently have ~30 words each) and trust signals like structured data and artist bios. Schema markup gives Mad Hudson first-mover advantage in a category no competitor has structured yet.

Five same-day wins cost nothing and change nothing visually. Entity building and AEO strategy position the brand for where search is going, not just where it is.

Five same-day fixes

None of these change how the site looks. All of them change how search engines see it.

  1. Add alt text to every product image
    Products → click image → Alt text
  2. Write custom meta descriptions
    Products → Search engine listing → Description
  3. Update page titles with artist names
    Same section → Page title
  4. Enable bot filtering
    Analytics → Settings → Exclude known bots
  5. Add UTM parameters to link-in-bio
    ?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=linkinbio
