Recommendations

Path Forward

Strongest Combinations

  • C1 (Hybrid) — Best of both: timer awareness + day position
  • G1 (Sunburst Depletion) — Strongest brand identity, mechanical viable
  • M3 (Color Disc) — Most novel mechanical, ambient time-feel

Number Integration Options

  • Subtle hour indices — Small markers at 12/3/6/9 only
  • Digital center — Precise time in center, visual system around
  • Removable bezel — Numbers on optional ring, swap for minimal

Mechanical Feasibility

  • Easiest: M1 (bezel) — uses standard movement + rotating bezel
  • Medium: M3 (disc) — disc complication exists, custom colors
  • Hardest: M2 (retrograde) — requires specialized movement

Prototype Priority

  • Quick test: Print dial inserts for blank watch case
  • E-ink: Watchy platform for C1/G2 validation
  • Mechanical: Source dive watch + custom bezel insert

Mechanical Design Notes

Rotating bezel (M1): Most accessible. 120-click unidirectional bezel standard in dive watches. Custom bezel insert with Girard-style graphics. Pairs with any automatic movement (Miyota 9015, Sellita SW200).

Color disc (M3): Day/night or time-of-day discs exist (see Nomos Autobahn). Would need custom disc with Girard palette. Movement with disc complication (ETA 2824 + module, or purpose-built).

Retrograde (M2): Beautiful but complex. Requires retrograde movement (Dubois Depraz modules). Higher cost, longer development. Consider for V2.

Hybrid approach: Analog hands for time + digital subdial for timer (Fossil-style). Proven reliable, lower barrier.
