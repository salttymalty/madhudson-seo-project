05 — THE OPEN QUESTION

B2B design services positioning

The team has B2B capability. The question is how to position it.

THE DILEMMA
Watch brand and design services share DNA — design thinking, production expertise, artist relationships
But they serve different audiences with different needs
Consumer buyers want a distinctive product. B2B clients want proof of capability.
Making design services visible could strengthen credibility — or dilute brand focus
WHAT RESEARCH SUGGESTS
Consumer brand as proof: watches demonstrate end-to-end capability for B2B pitches
Same story, different audiences: "Here's what we made. Here's what we can make for you."
Light endorsement model: Mad Hudson leads, "Mad Hudson Design" or "Mad Hudson Studios" as soft sub-brand
Single website, dual paths: /watches for consumers, /design for B2B clients

Structural Options

OPTION A
Single entity, dual audiences

One Mad Hudson LLC. Watches and design services under same roof. Website has both paths. Macy's project is portfolio piece, not separate business.

OPTION B
Holding company with operating LLCs

Mad Hudson Holdings → Mad Hudson Watches LLC + Mad Hudson Design LLC. Cleaner liability separation. More administrative overhead. Easier to sell or spin off one side later.

OPTION C
Stealth B2B

Don't publicly market design services. Let it grow through relationships. Watches are the public face. B2B work happens but isn't branded. Lowest complexity, limits B2B growth.

"Is Mad Hudson a design house that makes watches, or a watch company with design capability?"

What Research Says About Small Creative Startups

PATTERN
Founders burn out around year 4-5

Many "pretty successful" startups sell at about the same time — founders get burnt out, sell just as things are getting good. Not inevitable, but common.

PATTERN
Heroism doesn't scale

The biggest internal challenge is transitioning from startup heroism (whatever-it-takes) to more systematic processes. Many founders stay stuck in improvisation mode.

INSIGHT
Systems before hires

"You don't scale by hiring. You scale by systemizing." Adding people to broken systems multiplies inefficiency. Document workflows first, then decide what to delegate.

INSIGHT
Hire to fill gaps, not to clone

Double-down on founder strengths, hire to cover weaknesses. Creative founders often need operations support — but only when the systems are ready for someone else to run them.

TIMING
If workload is unsustainable, you should have hired already

Rule of thumb: if a task isn't high-level decision-making, someone else can probably do it. Start small — virtual assistant, fulfillment help, social scheduling.

07 — GROWTH CHANNELS

Watch development roadmap

Three channels worth exploring. Each requires different capabilities.

Corporate Gifting

HIGH-ROI
Executive gifts ($200-300 fits perfectly)

Companies seek unique, conversation-starting gifts. Hand-packaged experience aligns. Entry: employee milestones, client appreciation, deal closers.

FRICTION
Volume capacity + custom branding options

Requires dedicated outreach, potentially custom dial runs, faster turnaround than DTC.

Museum Gift Shops

FIT
Artist collaboration model is exactly what buyers want

Museums seek exclusive products with compelling artist stories. Not literal reproductions — products tied to their mission. Art Institute, MCA, design museums all potential fits.

FRICTION
Wholesale margins, exclusivity requests, consignment terms

Need to decide: are you willing to accept 50% margins? Can you do exclusive runs for specific institutions?

Brand Collaborations

POTENTIAL
Horizontal products with aligned brands

Kitchen timers, clocks, design objects. Leverage existing manufacturing relationships and design language. Partners: Areaware, HAY, Schoolhouse, indie coffee/food brands, music/audio brands.

FRICTION
Product development bandwidth, IP agreements, new manufacturing

Who leads this? Requires new capabilities or partner structure.
