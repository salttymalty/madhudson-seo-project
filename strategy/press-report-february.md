### **Angle 1: "Artist-as-Author" Model**
**Hook:** Unlike typical brand collaborations, Mad Hudson positions artists as authors‚Äînot accessories to the brand story. The watch tells *their* story, funded by their community.

**Best For:** It's Nice That, Designboom, Dezeen, Creative Review, Eye on Design, Design Milk
**Tone:** "Transparency meets artistry"
**Why It Works:** Fits design publication's interest in ethical design, emerging voices, and creative economics.

---

### **Angle 2: Limited Edition Culture & Scarcity as Meaning**
**Hook:** Pre-orders fund artist next work. 100-piece limits aren't about exclusivity‚Äîthey're about intent. Each watch is sustainable, artist-backed, and tied to real creativity.

**Best For:** Wallpaper*, Creative Review, DIELINE, Fast Company
**Tone:** "Design with purpose"
**Why It Works:** Aligns with luxury publications' interest in meaningful scarcity + sustainability + artist economics.

---

### **Angle 3: Independent Watch Brand Positioning**
**Hook:** In a market dominated by traditional watchmakers and tech companies, Mad Hudson offers a third way: watches *for* creators, made *with* artists, funded by community pre-orders.

**Best For:** Hodinkee, Worn & Wound, A Blog To Watch
**Tone:** "Authentic watchmaking meets artist community"
**Why It Works:** Watch media loves authentic stories. Mad Hudson's artist-centric model differentiates from competitors.

---

### **Angle 4: Creator Economy & Transparent Economics**
**Hook:** Show backer economics: How much of revenue goes to artist? What does pre-order funding enable? How does community shape product?

**Best For:** Fast Company, The Verge, Eye on Design
**Tone:** "The future of creator funding"
**Why It Works:** Aligns with publication's coverage of emerging business models, creator platforms, and transparent supply chains.

---

### **Angle 5: Design Process & Craftsmanship**
**Hook:** Deep dive into how an artist (e.g., Brad Podray, Bryce Wong) designed their specific watch. What inspired? What material choices? Why this collaboration?

**Best For:** Designboom, Dezeen, Wallpaper*, Design Milk, DIELINE
**Tone:** "The artistry behind the dial"
**Why It Works:** Design publications love process stories. Shows authentic collaboration + creative thinking.
