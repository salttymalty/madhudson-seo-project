Strategic Insights

▼

The Instagram Funnel is Broken, Not Instagram Itself

Brad drives real attention — 60K+ sessions from Instagram. But the path from IG to purchase has a gap. The mismatch between Instagram experience (personality, energy, story) and product page experience (sparse, transactional) is the kill zone. 0.038% conversion rate says the traffic is good but the landing isn't.

The Unattributed $28.3K Needs UTM Detective Work

Add UTM parameters to link-in-bio (?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=bio_link&utm_campaign=profile). Within 1 month you'll know if the "Direct" bucket is actually Instagram visitors with stripped referrers. If so, Instagram isn't #2 — it's #1, and the entire prioritization shifts.

Seasonality is Plannable

27% of revenue in Nov/Dec isn't a surprise — it's a target. Everything ready by October 2026. This is a hard deadline, not a soft one. The marginal value of improvements drops 73% after December.

Merch Validates the Brand

Mad Cap: 95% sell-through. Caps and tees selling well. The brand identity works — people want to wear it. This validates that the issue isn't brand appeal, it's discovery and trust.

Prioritize Content for Slow Movers

Dogma (282 units, 5% sell-through), Eddie (92 units, 8%), Marshall (95 units, 5%), Miami (97 units, 3%) = 566 watches, potentially $90K+ in tied-up inventory. These need content first. They're not selling because people don't find them or don't understand why they should want them.

Inventory Health Alert: Slow movers represent significant capital tied up. Content expansion isn't just an SEO play — it's inventory management. Moving these watches at even 20-30% sell-through would free up $18K-$27K in working capital.

Key Assumptions and What Could Change Them

▼

All models rest on assumptions. Here's what this one assumes, and what evidence would shift the strategy.

  • All conversion lift estimates use industry benchmarks, not measured on this site. Once improvements are live, measure actual lift. If trust signals produce 50% lift instead of 32%, the $8K-$17K becomes $12K-$26K.
  • Bot traffic estimate is based on correlation analysis, not definitive identification. If bot filtering reveals 50% bot traffic instead of 30%, organic traffic numbers drop — but so does server load and analytics noise.
  • "Unattributed" bucket may contain Instagram traffic. UTM will reveal this. If Instagram is actually #1 revenue channel (not #2), strategy shifts toward fixing that funnel first.
  • If Instagram is actually #1, the priority order inverts. Instagram conversion path moves from "strategic/compounding" to "do this first." Trust signals stay high priority (they help all channels).
  • Product content ROI assumes Google eventually indexes and ranks expanded pages. Typical lag: 2-4 weeks. If indexing is delayed (site speed, crawl budget issues), ROI timeline shifts right.

What to measure after 30 days:

  • UTM data from Instagram link-in-bio (what % of "Direct" was actually Instagram?)
  • Google Search Console impressions + clicks (did meta descriptions improve CTR?)
  • Product page engagement (time on page, scroll depth — proxy for content quality)
  • Conversion rate by channel (did trust signals move the needle?)

Implementation Checklist

▼

Ordered by priority — start at the top, work down.

  • Enable bot filtering in Shopify Analytics (Settings → Analytics → "Filter out bots")
  • Add UTM parameters to Instagram link-in-bio (?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=bio_link)
  • Implement trust signals (guarantee badge, Shop Pay, review prompts)
  • Expand product content (slow movers first: Dogma, Eddie, Marshall, Miami)
  • Deploy schema markup (Artist, Product, LimitedEdition, materials)
  • Fix Instagram conversion path (landing pages, mobile UX, trust signals)
  • Track results after 30 days — compare measured lift vs. projections
