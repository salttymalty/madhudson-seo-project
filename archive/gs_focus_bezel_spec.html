Product Specification

Focus Bezel

Detailed specification for manufacturing. Phase 1 product.

01 — Product Overview

Concept

A dive-watch-style rotating bezel repurposed for attention, not oxygen. The wearer rotates the bezel to align a colored sector with the minute hand, marking the start of a focus session. When the minute hand exits the sector, the session is complete.

One-Line Description

"It's just a dive timer, but warmer and for your attention."

Terracotta Sector

Primary colorway. Warm, grounded.

Gold Sector

Alternate colorway. Softer warmth.

Teal Sector

Cool variant. Calm focus.

02 — Bezel Insert Specification

Bezel Insert Details

Focus Sector

  • Arc: 150° (25 minutes)
  • Position: Starts at 12 o'clock (0 marker)
  • End marker: Bold white line at 25-minute position
  • Color: Solid fill, no gradient

Minute Markers

  • Major ticks: Every 5 minutes (bold)
  • Minor ticks: Every minute (lighter, optional)
  • Numerals: 0, 25, 30, 45 only
  • 0 marker: White, bold, with lume pip

Materials

  • Insert: Anodized aluminum or ceramic
  • Print: Pad-printed or laser-etched markers
  • Lume: C3 SuperLuminova on 0 pip and hands

Color Specifications

Terracotta #c45d3a
Gold #d4a044
Teal #4a8a8a
Bezel body #3a3632
Dial cream #f5f0e8

Why 25 minutes? Common focus session length. Long enough for deep work, short enough to feel achievable. Not branded as "pomodoro" — just a useful default. Future variants could offer 15, 30, or 45-minute sectors.

04 — User Experience Flow

Starting a Focus Session

  1. Glance at watch — Note where minute hand currently points
  2. Rotate bezel — Align the colored sector's start (0 marker) with current minute hand position
  3. Feel the click — Tactile confirmation that session has begun
  4. Work — No need to watch the watch; time is held
  5. Session complete — When minute hand exits the colored sector, the session is done

Key Affordances

  • Unidirectional bezel: Can only rotate counterclockwise, preventing accidental extension
  • Tactile feedback: Each click = 30 seconds, allowing fine adjustment
  • Visual boundary: Clear start/end markers on sector
  • No counting: Position-based, not number-based

Why this works: Unlike a timer that counts down, the bezel creates a spatial container for time. You're not watching numbers decrease — you're watching your hand travel through a defined space. When it exits, you're done. No buzzing, no alarms, no interruption.
