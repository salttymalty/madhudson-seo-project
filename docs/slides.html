Today

Same-Day Checklist

You can do all five in a single afternoon. Nothing about your site will look different to customers.

  • Add alt text to every product image
    Products → click each image → Alt text field. Include watch name, artist, visual description.
    Opens Google Image Search traffic + improves accessibility
  • Write custom meta descriptions
    Products → "Search engine listing preview" → Description. Template: "[Watch] — limited edition by [Artist]. [N] pieces. Free shipping."
    Controls what appears below your page title in Google search results
  • Update page titles with artist names
    Same section → Page title → "[Watch] by [Artist] — Limited Edition Artist Watch | Mad Hudson"
    Most prominent text in search results. Captures artist-name queries.
  • Enable bot filtering
    Analytics → Settings → toggle "Exclude known bots"
    Reveals your true baseline. Current data mixes Brad's real social traffic with bot amplification.
  • Add UTM parameters to Instagram link-in-bio
    Change the link-in-bio URL to include ?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=linkinbio
    $28K in orders (62%) have no tracked source. UTM tags solve the attribution blindspot in one month.

AEO

How Answer Engines Choose What to Cite

Gate 1: Fetchable

Can the model access the page? JavaScript-rendered content gets skipped. LLMs are sensitive to compute cost — they bypass complex sites. Clean HTML wins.

Mad Hudson: Needs Shopify theme verification

Gate 2: Choosable

Does the page answer the query? Answer engines rewrite "best unique watches" into 3-4 shorter queries. Pages that match these fan-out queries get chosen for deeper analysis.

Mad Hudson: Needs "best" keyword alignment

Gate 3: Extractable

Can the model clip a clean chunk? It needs a quotable 1-3 sentence passage — not a wall of text. Current product pages have ~30 words of generic copy. Not extractable.

Mad Hudson: Draft extractable copy ready

Fan-out flattens everything to "best." Whoever owns "best artist collaboration watches" wins the citation.
