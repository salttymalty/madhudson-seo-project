Internal Strategy

Gs Watch — Analog-First Strategy

Reframed concepts, language system, manufacturing roadmap. Aligned with brand philosophy.

01 — Reconciliation: E-ink Concepts → Analog Execution

Our exploratory work developed strong concepts (radial rings for day orientation, depletion visualization for timers, haptic time cues). However, the execution path drifted toward a smartwatch form factor that conflicts with the brand's analog-first philosophy.

This document reframes those concepts for analog-first execution.

Concept Translation

E-ink Concept Analog Execution Product
Depletion Arc (timer countdown) Rotating bezel with colored sector Focus Bezel
Radial Rings (day remaining) Printed dial zones + hour hand position Day Map
Timer bar + digital countdown Chronograph subdial with focus sector Studio Chrono
Haptic cuckoo (hourly vibration) Small e-ink subdial + optional haptic Anchor Window (later phase)

Key shift: Analog hands remain the primary time-telling interface. Any digital element is supplementary context, not a replacement for the watch face.

02 — Analog Concepts: Detailed Specifications

Focus Bezel

The core product. A dive-watch-style rotating bezel repurposed for attention, not oxygen.

Mechanism

  • Standard 3-hand quartz or automatic movement
  • Unidirectional rotating bezel (60 clicks)
  • Colored sector on bezel marks focus duration
  • User rotates bezel to align sector start with minute hand
  • When minute hand exits sector, session is complete

Experience

"Twist to begin. The watch holds your focus window. When the hand exits the color, you're done."

Manufacturing Notes

Component Specification Complexity
Movement Miyota 9039 (auto) or 2035 (quartz) Low — standard supply
Case 38-40mm, 316L steel or recycled aluminum Low — standard tooling
Bezel Aluminum insert, 60-click unidirectional Low — dive watch standard
Bezel insert Custom print with 25-min colored sector Low — print/anodize
Dial Minimal indices, warm cream/white Low

Day Map

Orientation over precision. The dial shows where you are in your day, not just what time it is.

Mechanism

  • Standard 2-hand or 3-hand movement
  • Dial printed with colored zones (dawn, morning, midday, afternoon, evening, night)
  • Hour hand position shows current zone at a glance
  • No numbers required — color = orientation

Experience

"Glance at your wrist. You're in the morning field. The afternoon coast is ahead. No math, just position."

Zone Calibration Options

Option Zone Layout Best For
Standard (6-zone) Dawn / Morning / Midday / Afternoon / Evening / Night General use
Early Riser (shifted) Zones shifted earlier (dawn starts at 4am position) Morning people
Night Owl (shifted) Zones shifted later (morning extends to noon position) Late schedules
Quarterly (4-zone) Morning / Afternoon / Evening / Night Simpler read

Tone note: Avoid language like "optimize your day zones" or "track your productivity across zones." Position as orientation and rhythm, not measurement.

Studio Chrono

A meca-quartz chronograph with one subdial tuned to focus sessions. The tactile pusher click begins a work block.

Key Features

  • Meca-quartz movement (Seiko VK63 or similar) — sweeping chrono hand, quartz reliability
  • 30-minute subdial with bold 25-minute sector highlighted
  • Pusher at 2 o'clock starts/stops; pusher at 4 o'clock resets
  • Main dial remains a functional time-telling watch

Experience

"Click. The hand sweeps. When it crosses the bold line, the block is done. A stopwatch for making things."

Complexity

Medium. Meca-quartz movements are available but require more case engineering (pushers, gaskets). Higher cost than Focus Bezel. Consider as second product after Focus Bezel proves demand.

03 — Language System

A consistent vocabulary for use across products, straps, and marketing. Words should feel warm, grounded, and intelligent — never clinical, hustle-culture, or appropriative.

anchor

A chosen moment that matters. A point of orientation in the day.

Use: "Set your anchor." "The watch holds your anchor."

Avoid: "productivity anchor," "time anchor"

field

A zone or period of the day. Implies open space, not rigid blocks.

Use: "morning field," "evening field," "transition field"

Avoid: "time block," "productivity zone"

map

The dial as orientation device. Shows where you are, not what you must do.

Use: "Day Map," "mapping your rhythm"

Avoid: "time map," "schedule map"

companion

The watch as a gentle presence, not a monitor or tracker.

Use: "a quiet companion," "companion app" (for setup only)

Avoid: "assistant," "coach," "guide"

ritual

Repeated actions with care and intention. Charging, winding, strap care.

Use: "charging ritual," "care ritual," "morning ritual"

Avoid: "routine," "habit," "practice" (too productivity-coded)

session

A bounded period of focus. Has a beginning and end.

Use: "focus session," "studio session"

Avoid: "sprint," "block," "pomodoro" (too productivity-coded)

Phrases to Avoid

Avoid Why Replace With
"Time blindness support" Implies fixing a deficit "Gentle orientation" or "finding your place in the day"
"ND-focused" / "ADHD-friendly" Medicalizes; risks overclaiming "For different relationships with time"
"Optimize your time" Hustle culture "Orient in your day"
"Indigenous time concepts" Appropriative without collaboration "Cyclical rhythms" or "relational time"
"Productivity system" Self-optimization framing "A way of being with time"
"Track your focus" Surveillance framing "Hold your focus" or "mark a session"
"Urgency zone" Anxiety-inducing "Transition field" or "evening coast"

Litmus test: Would this language make someone with ADHD feel seen and supported, or judged and pathologized? Would it make anyone feel they're broken and need fixing? If yes, rewrite.

04 — Manufacturing Roadmap

Phase 1: Focus Bezel

First product. Lowest risk. Proves core concept.

Standard dive watch construction. Off-the-shelf movement. Custom bezel insert is only unique component. Can use existing case tooling from HK partners with minor modifications.

Est. MOQ: 300-500 | Est. unit cost: $45-65 | Timeline: 3-4 months

Phase 2: Day Map

Second product. Tests zone dial concept.

Same case and movement as Focus Bezel. Unique component is printed dial with zone colors. Could offer as variant dial for same case (reduces tooling).

Est. additional cost: Dial printing setup | Timeline: +1-2 months after Phase 1

Phase 3: Studio Chrono

Third product. Higher complexity. Validates premium tier.

Requires meca-quartz movement, pusher engineering, larger case. Consider only after Focus Bezel/Day Map prove demand. Could partner with existing chrono manufacturer to reduce tooling.

Est. unit cost: $80-120 | Timeline: 6-8 months

Phase 4: Anchor Window Hybrid

Later-phase. Limited run. Internal R&D continues.

Analog movement + small e-ink subdial. Requires custom PCB, battery management, companion app. Only pursue after analog line is profitable and brand is established.

Est. unit cost: $150-250 | Timeline: 12-18 months | Run: Limited (500-1000)

Strap Manufacturing

Recycled Ocean Nylon

Lead strap. Widely available from sustainable suppliers. Quick-release hardware standard. Multiple colorways.

Est. cost: $8-12/unit

Cork + Textile Hybrid

More specialized. Cork suppliers in Portugal. Requires custom layering. Distinctive texture and patina.

Est. cost: $15-22/unit

Raw Denim

Selvedge denim sourced from mills. Requires edge binding. Fades uniquely over time. Workwear aesthetic.

Est. cost: $12-18/unit

Plant-Based Leather (Cactus)

Premium option. Desserto or similar supplier. Dressier look while maintaining vegan ethics.

Est. cost: $20-30/unit

05 — Anchor Window Hybrid: Internal Spec

Later-phase concept. Not for initial launch. Included here for continuity with exploratory work.

Core Principle

Analog timekeeping is primary. The digital element is a small "window" that provides quiet context about the day — not a screen, not a UI, not a smartwatch.

Allowed Behaviors

  • Display current day zone (dawn/morning/midday/afternoon/evening/night)
  • Show timer countdown when active (user-initiated via crown or button)
  • Sync with calendar for 1-3 user-selected "anchor" events (therapy, rehearsal, pickup)
  • Subtle indicator when anchor is approaching or active
  • Optional gentle haptic at hour, half-hour, or anchor time (default off)

Forbidden Behaviors

  • Notifications of any kind (messages, email, social)
  • Fitness tracking, step counts, heart rate, rings, streaks
  • Multiple screens, menus, scrolling, gestures
  • Bright display, frequent updates, anything that invites checking
  • Behavior that requires the app to function day-to-day

Technical Architecture

Component Specification
Analog movement Standard quartz 3-hand (Miyota or similar)
Digital subdial ~0.5" circular e-ink segment, ultra-low power
Microcontroller ESP32-S3 or nRF52 (BLE for sync)
Battery CR2032 or small LiPo, 3-6 week target life
Haptic Small ERM or LRA motor, optional use
Companion app Setup and config only; no daily engagement

Graceful Degradation

If the digital module loses power or fails:

  • Analog movement continues to tell time correctly
  • Watch remains fully functional as an analog timepiece
  • E-ink subdial shows last state (static, but not blank)
  • User can continue using watch indefinitely without digital features

Key test: If someone wore this watch for a month without ever syncing or using the digital features, would it still feel like a complete, satisfying watch? The answer must be yes.
