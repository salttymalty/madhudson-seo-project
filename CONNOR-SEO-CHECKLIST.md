## PHASE 1: This Week (5-6 hours total)

**Strategy:** Tell artist-as-author stories. Stop competing for generic "watch" keywords.

### Assign & Track These 7 Quick Wins

#### QUICK WIN #1: Product Page Narratives (3 hours)
- [ ] **Task:** Expand all 6 product descriptions (2-3 sentences → 500-1000 words)
- **Pages:** Eddie, Marshall, Miami, Broken Time, CD Watch, Dogma
- **Content needed per watch:**
  - Artist bio (50-100 words): Who they are, their practice
  - Collaboration story (150-250 words): How partnership happened, what inspired them
  - Design process (150-250 words): Materials, inspiration, decisions
  - Why this artist (100-150 words): What's authentic about this collab
  - Pre-order transparency (50-100 words): Why 100 pieces, why pre-order matters to artist
  - 2-3 artist photos with descriptive alt text
- **Assign to:** Copywriter (or you, with artist input for quotes/stories)
- **Why:** Moves product pages from specs to storytelling. Targets "watches for creators," "artist-made watches"
- **Done when:** Each page has 500+ words, includes all 5 sections above, artist names hyperlinked

---

#### QUICK WIN #2: Meta Descriptions & H1 Tags (1 hour)
- [ ] **Task:** Update H1s and meta descriptions on all 6 product pages
- **What to change:**
  - H1: From "[Watch Name]" → "[Artist Name] × [Watch Name] | Limited Edition Artist Collaboration"
  - Meta description (155-160 chars): Include artist name + "artist-made" or "watches for creators" + pre-order angle
- **Examples:**
  - H1: "Brad Podray × Eddie Watch | Limited Edition Artist Collaboration"
  - Meta: "Brad Podray's Eddie Watch: Artist-designed, limited edition pre-order. Where creative culture meets functional design."
- **Assign to:** Copywriter (20 min) or Dev (if pulling from product template)
- **Why:** H1 signals topic to Google. Meta descriptions drive CTR + keyword visibility
- **Done when:** All 6 product pages have updated H1s + meta descriptions

---

#### QUICK WIN #3: Artist/Creator Schema Markup (1 hour)
- [ ] **Task:** Add Person + Product schema to all 6 product pages
- **What to implement:**
  ```
  Person schema: Artist name, bio, image, URL (for artist profile pages we'll build in Phase 2)
  Product schema: Include "creator" field with artist name + "PreOrder" availability status
  ```
- **Assign to:** Dev (add to Shopify template, validate with Google Rich Results Test)
- **Why:** Helps Google understand artist-product relationship. Enables rich snippets in search results
- **Done when:** Schema validates with no errors in Google Rich Results Test

---

#### QUICK WIN #4: Collection Page Intros (1.5 hours)
- [ ] **Task:** Write 100-200 word thematic intros for main collections
- **Collections to update:**
  - New Releases
  - Limited Editions
  - By artist (if you have separate collections)
- **What each intro includes:**
  - What unites this collection? (artist, theme, design approach)
  - Who are these for? (fans of [artist], collectors of [theme], people who value [value])
  - 2-3 internal links to related blog posts or artist profiles
- **Assign to:** Copywriter
- **Why:** Collection pages often ignored. Thematic intros reduce bounce, enable internal linking
- **Done when:** 3-4 collections each have 100+ word intro with 2+ internal links

---

#### QUICK WIN #5: Artist Spotlights Landing Page (1.5 hours)
- [ ] **Task:** Create "/stories" or "/artist-spotlights" landing page
- **Page includes:**
  - Hero: "Stories from the Artists Behind the Watches"
  - Subheading: "Limited edition watches where artists are authors, not collaborators"
  - 150-200 word intro explaining artist storytelling focus
  - CTA button: "Read the latest artist interview"
  - Grid of 3+ artist spotlights with images, names, brief intros (50 words each)
  - Link from: Main nav, product pages, footer
- **Assign to:** Designer (page layout) + Copywriter (copy) + Dev (Shopify setup)
- **Why:** Signals to Google that artist storytelling is core to brand. Hub for blog traffic (Phase 2)
- **Done when:** Page is live, linked from 3+ locations, includes artist spotlights grid

---

#### QUICK WIN #6: Breadcrumb Schema (30 min)
- [ ] **Task:** Add breadcrumb schema markup to product + collection pages
- **What it does:** Helps Google understand site structure. Can increase CTR in search results
- **Assign to:** Dev
- **Done when:** Schema validates in Google Rich Results Test

---

#### QUICK WIN #7: FAQ + FAQ Schema (1.5 hours)
- [ ] **Task:** Create FAQ page (or section) about pre-orders + artist support
- **Questions to answer:**
  - How do pre-orders work?
  - Why limited edition (why not make more)?
  - How long until my watch ships?
  - What's the artist's involvement in each watch?
  - Do you ship internationally? (highlight 28+ countries)
  - Why are pre-orders better than traditional retail?
  - Can I cancel my pre-order?
- **Each answer:** 100-150 words, user-focused
- **Add FAQ schema markup** (Dev will implement)
- **Assign to:** Copywriter (copy) + Dev (schema)
- **Why:** Targets "how to" keywords. Can earn featured snippet placement. Answers buyer objections
- **Done when:** FAQ has 5+ Q&A pairs, schema validates
