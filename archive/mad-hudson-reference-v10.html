What This Is

This Document

A reflection tool for founders — not a pitch deck, not a business plan. It surfaces questions, organizes current thinking, and provides a place to track what's known and unknown. Use it for solo planning, partner alignment conversations, or advisor context. Update it as things change.

None of this is prescriptive. Some of it is probably wrong — AI-assisted research has blind spots, and outsider perspectives carry inherent hubris. Treat it as a starting point to poke around with, not a roadmap.

The Clarity Question

Can you explain the relationship between watches and design services in one sentence? If not, that's worth solving before scaling either lane.

  • Are they the same business with two revenue streams, or two businesses sharing founders?
  • Does design services work inform watch design, or are they separate creative tracks?
  • Would you turn down a design project to focus on a watch release? Vice versa?
  • If one lane disappeared tomorrow, would the other be stronger or weaker?
  • Is design services meant to fund watches until watches sustain themselves?

Operations

Where automation or support could reduce friction without losing the handmade feel. (High-ROI priorities are at the top of this document.)

Automation Opportunities

  • Social scheduling: Batch content creation, schedule posts. ~5 hrs/week recovered for ~$15/month.
  • Order notifications: Automated shipping updates, delivery confirmations. Reduces "where's my order?" inquiries.
  • Review requests: Post-purchase flow asking for reviews at the right moment. Builds social proof passively.
  • Abandoned cart recovery: 5–15% recovery rate is typical. Money left on the table without it.
  • Restock alerts: For repeat customers or waitlist items. Low effort, high intent.

None of these require hiring. Most are one-time setup with occasional maintenance.

Support Considerations

  • Bookkeeping: Part-time or fractional. Keeps financials clean, frees founder time, essential before any serious scaling.
  • Fulfillment help: When does hand-packaging stop being a brand feature and start being a bottleneck? What's the threshold?
  • Customer service overflow: Contract or part-time for peak periods (launches, holidays). Protect founder energy.

First hire is usually the task you hate, not the task you love. Creative founders over-index on creative roles.

Research Pathways

Shopify app ecosystem for automation (Klaviyo, Gorgias, Loop Returns). What similar-sized brands actually use vs. what gets recommended. True cost of apps when stacked. When to systematize vs. when ad hoc is fine.

Protection

Not urgent, but know your triggers.

Questions to Answer

  • Do you have written agreements with each artist covering IP ownership, royalties, and usage rights?
  • Is "Mad Hudson" trademarked? In what classes?
  • If a customer sued over a product defect, what's the liability exposure?
  • Does the LLC operating agreement address the two-division structure?

Triggers for Restructuring

  • Design services revenue exceeds 30% of total
  • A buyer expresses interest in one division
  • A legal or liability event in one division threatens the other
  • One founder wants out
  • B2B contracts require dedicated entity

Tariffs & Manufacturing (2025)

  • De minimis elimination: No more duty-free under $800 for China/HK. Now 90% tariff or $75 flat fee on low-value packages.
  • DIY Watch Club case study: 80% US revenue, pivoted to Europe after tariffs. Market share there went 6% → 30%. US revenue impact: 20–30%.
  • Industry response: Supply chain diversification (India emerging), batch ordering, tariff engineering (component sourcing), price increases.
  • Hong Kong Watch Fair: Still valuable for meeting manufacturers, understanding component landscape.

Email & Retention

Email generates $36 revenue per $1 spent — highest ROI channel. Core flows for DTC:

  • Welcome series: Can go from $0.50 to $8 per recipient with proper setup.
  • Abandoned cart: 5–15% recovery typical. One SMS in flow can accelerate.
  • Post-purchase: Reviews, referrals, repeat purchase timing.
  • Browse abandonment: Often missing from small brand setups.

Target: ~30% email-attributed revenue, split evenly between flows and campaigns.
