Mechanical Implementation Paths

A. Rotating Bezel + Subdial

Most accessible. Uses existing dive watch mechanics. Bezel = timer (user-set). Subdial = radial visualization (printed or with single hand). Standard automatic movement. Seiko/Citizen base calibers work.

B. Regulator Style

Separate hour/minute subdials (regulator layout). Center area free for radial now visualization. Timer via crown-operated retrograde hand. Higher movement complexity, stronger differentiation.

C. Quartz Hybrid

Analog hands + small e-ink or LCD segment for timer countdown. Seiko/Citizen style hybrid movement. Best of both: mechanical feel, digital precision for timer. Proven commercial path.

D. Full E-ink

Custom e-ink display. All visualizations programmable. Multiple faces. Watchy platform ($55-70) for prototyping. Longest battery, most flexible. Software-defined, hardware later.

Recommendations

Primary: Hybrid + Numbers — Combines conventional legibility with ND visualization. Outer depletion arc handles timer. Inner radial rings handle day-position. Analog hands provide familiar reading. Appeals to both ND users and broader market.

Secondary: Rotating Bezel Mechanical — Most manufacturable physical watch. Leverages existing dive watch infrastructure. Timer = physical bezel interaction (tactile, satisfying). Subdial carries radial now in miniature.

Girard integration:

  • Apply Girard palette across all variants (not just dedicated "Girard" faces)
  • Sunburst rays as hour markers (replaces conventional indices)
  • Color progression: dawn→night around dial edge
  • Limited edition Girard-forward variant for brand differentiation

Number strategy:

  • Minimal markers: 12-3-6-9 only (reduces clutter)
  • Optional digital subdisplay for precise time (hybrid quartz)
  • Numbers as fallback — train eye toward pattern reading over time

Prototype path:

  • Watchy e-ink for software concept validation
  • Blank dive watch case + printed dial for mechanical feel test
  • 3D printed bezel inserts for timer marking exploration
